 The Antioch school shooter partially streamed the Nashville incident live.



Two Dead, One Injured in Antioch High School Shooting

A tragic shooting at Antioch High School in Nashville on Wednesday morning left two students dead, including the shooter, and another student injured. The incident took place in the school cafeteria at approximately 11:09 a.m.

Metro Nashville Police Department (MNPD) spokesperson Don Aaron reported that law enforcement received the first emergency call just two minutes after a 17-year-old student began firing.

Authorities confirmed that the shooter fatally wounded one student before taking his own life. A third student suffered a minor gunshot wound, while a fourth student sustained a facial injury from a fall but was not hit by gunfire. The injured students were transported to Vanderbilt University Medical Center for treatment.

On Wednesday afternoon, MNPD identified the deceased victim as 16-year-old Josselin Corea Escalante and the shooter as 17-year-old Solomon Henderson. The identities of the other students involved are still being determined.

Tragedy Strikes Nearly Two Years After The Covenant School Shooting

This shooting follows a deadly attack nearly two years ago at The Covenant School in Nashville, where three children and three staff members were killed before the shooter was fatally shot by police.

Antioch High School Basketball Games Canceled

As a precautionary measure, Antioch High School’s scheduled boys' and girls' basketball games against Hillsboro High School on Friday have been canceled, according to Metro Nashville Public Schools Athletics Director Mark North.

Shooter’s Online Activity Reveals Troubling Content

Investigators uncovered a 300-page manifesto attributed to the Antioch High School shooter. The document included references to alt-right ideology, explicit praise for Adolf Hitler, and statements against racial integration. It also contained violent rhetoric expressing a desire for revenge against society, as well as images from past school shootings, according to MNPD Chief John Drake.

Authorities linked the shooter to various social media accounts across multiple platforms, including X, Kick, and TikTok. These accounts reportedly contained extremist and misogynistic content associated with white nationalist and "incel" communities.

The document, last updated four hours before the attack, indicated that the shooter originally planned to carry out the attack on Thursday. It also mentioned an intent to kill at least ten people, including a specific elementary school teacher who was not named.

Community Gathers for Vigil

In response to the tragedy, community members, students, and faith leaders assembled Wednesday night at Hamilton United Methodist Church in Antioch. The vigil was interfaith and multilingual, bringing together a diverse group of attendees seeking comfort and solidarity.

Among the speakers were Nashville Mayor Freddie O’Connell, Metro Nashville Councilmembers Delishia Porterfield, Deonté Harrell, and Zulfat Suara. Clergy members, including Revs. Quentin Dickerson, Mamdouh Ramzy, Rachel Booker, and Imam Moaz Roaf from the Islamic Center of Tennessee, also shared words of encouragement.

Aaron Rodriguez, a teacher at Antioch High School, moved many attendees to tears with his speech. Addressing parents in the audience, he assured them, “Your children are brave, and they did everything right.”

Many students and grief counselors attended the vigil, supporting each other through their grief. Councilmember Porterfield emphasized unity, stating, “When we hold hands, when we pray together…we acknowledge we are stronger together.”

Investigation into the Shooting Continues

Authorities are still working to determine a motive for the shooting, which resulted in the deaths of 16-year-old Josselin Corea Escalante and 17-year-old Solomon Henderson. A 17-year-old male student who was wounded has since been treated and released from Vanderbilt University Medical Center.

MNPD has not yet found any clear connections between Henderson and the students who were shot, and investigators believe they may have been struck at random inside the cafeteria.

At the time of the attack, two school resource officers were present on campus but were in a different area of the building. By the time they arrived at the scene, Henderson had already turned the gun on himself.

Detectives are reviewing Henderson’s online posts and writings in collaboration with state and federal agencies. Authorities have not disclosed whether Henderson had any prior interactions with law enforcement.

Shooter Livestreamed Part of the Attack

Kick, a live-streaming platform, confirmed that Henderson broadcasted part of the shooting on his account. In a statement posted on X, the company said, “KICK rapidly banned the account and removed the video. Violence has no place on KICK. We are actively working with law enforcement and taking all appropriate steps to support their investigation.”

Some video clips circulating online show Henderson filming himself walking through school hallways and attempting to enter locked classrooms.

Gun Safety Concerns Raised

Dr. Jonathan Metzl, a sociology professor at Vanderbilt University and director of the Center for Medicine, Health, and Society, expressed concern over Tennessee’s ongoing refusal to implement stricter gun laws.

“We continue to see these tragedies in places where people should feel safe—schools, restaurants, and other public spaces,” Metzl said. “We need to learn from these incidents and implement policies that both protect the rights of gun owners and make schools safer.”

Metzl, author of What We’ve Become: Living and Dying in a Country of Arms, has previously proposed measures such as "red flag" laws and expanded background checks, but these initiatives have not gained traction in Tennessee’s legislature.

Antioch High School Closes for the Week

Metro Nashville Public Schools Director Adrienne Battle announced that Antioch High School will remain closed for the rest of the week to allow students and staff time to process the tragedy.

MNPS is arranging counseling services for students and faculty, though specific details on the location and availability of these resources have yet to be provided.

MNPD Chief: Online Materials Being Reviewed

During a news conference Wednesday afternoon, MNPD Chief John Drake confirmed that online materials connected to the shooter are playing a significant role in the ongoing investigation.

Authorities are still working to determine the shooter's motive, according to Chief John Drake.

"We're investigating that aspect," Drake stated. "There are online materials we're reviewing as part of the case."

He later emphasized the importance of vigilance in preventing such incidents.

"We suspect that some concerning materials were available, and perhaps they were noticed," Drake said. "If someone had spoken up, maybe this could have been prevented."

Metro Nashville Police Identify Victim and Shooter

Metro Nashville Police identified the victim who lost her life as 16-year-old Josselin Corea Escalante and the shooter as 17-year-old Solomon Henderson. The department shared the information on X, formerly Twitter, around 3:30 p.m. Wednesday.

Students and Parents Recall the Tragic Day

Kelisha Walden endured hours of waiting before she could finally embrace her daughter, Yvelle Walden, 16. Tears welled in her eyes as they reunited on Thursday.

For Yvelle, the day began like any other. She attended basketball practice before her morning classes and was heading to lunch when gunshots suddenly rang out.

Her lunch break began at 11:05 a.m.

"Out of nowhere, I just heard gunfire," she recounted.

Frightened, she rushed back to class and told her teacher to lock the door. More than three hours later, she was able to reunite with her mother.

"She ran up and told me not to cry," Kelisha recalled. "I told her: 'Too late.'"

Another student, Edwin Vanegas, 16, walked with a group of classmates to the designated reunification site. Instead of waiting for his family, he opted to meet up with friends before heading home, assuring his loved ones he was safe and didn’t need to be picked up.

Due to the timing of the incident, school officials decided that students who typically take the bus would follow their usual transportation schedule. Those who were car riders, like Vanegas, walked to the reunification site.

Nashville Council Members Respond

Two at-large members of Nashville's city council, Quin Evans Segall and Delishia Porterfield, addressed the tragedy on Bluesky Wednesday afternoon.

Porterfield expressed her condolences to the victims, their families, and those affected by the incident. She voiced concerns about Tennessee’s gun laws.

"This is another heartbreaking example of how unrestricted firearm access continues to endanger our communities," she stated.

She reaffirmed her commitment to establishing an Office of Youth Safety to address gun violence.

"We will not stop working to make our community safer," Porterfield added.

Evans Segall also shared her sorrow and frustration, stating that the tragedy was avoidable.

"This didn’t have to happen," she said. "We could have taken action to prevent it—but we didn’t."

White House Monitoring Situation

The White House confirmed that President Donald Trump and his team were tracking the developments at Antioch High School.

In an official statement, the White House extended thoughts and prayers to those affected and expressed gratitude to first responders for their swift action.

Teacher Used ‘Moana’ to Comfort Students

Special education teacher Chirelle Teufert described the experience as "organized chaos."

As law enforcement worked to secure the scene, she kept her classroom doors locked and blinds shut. To keep her students calm, she played the Disney film "Moana."

Teufert praised her students for their composure and dedicated herself to helping reunite them with their families. "It takes teamwork," she said, "and we have that."

School District Director Addresses the Tragedy

Metro Nashville Public Schools Director Adrienne Battle called it a "dark day" for the school and the entire community.

"My heart breaks for these families experiencing unimaginable loss," she said.

Battle mentioned that Antioch High had security measures in place, such as resource officers, secured entryways, and surveillance cameras with weapon-detection software. She reflected on her own past, recalling a school shooting that occurred when she was a student in 1994.

She commended the school's staff for their quick response. "Their actions were heroic on such a devastating day," she said.

Investigation Continues Overnight

Chief Drake revealed that the shooter, a 17-year-old male student, arrived at school on the bus before heading to the cafeteria, where he confronted and shot a 16-year-old female student.

He then fired multiple rounds before turning the gun on himself.

A 17-year-old male student suffered a grazing wound and was treated at Vanderbilt University Medical Center before being released.

After the shooting, SWAT teams searched the premises for additional victims, but none were found. The motive remains unknown, and the investigation is expected to continue into the early morning hours.

Students Recall Moments of Fear

Tinashae Smith, a ninth-grader, described the terrifying experience of hiding in her classroom. When she saw people running in the hallways, she alerted her teacher, who tried calling the administration but received no response.

After checking their phones, students discovered that a shooting had taken place. A lockdown was initiated, and they barricaded the door with desks and chairs.

"I was scared and confused," Smith said. "Everything happened so fast."

At around 2 p.m., she was finally able to reunite with her sister. "I never want to go through something like this again," she said.

Transportation and Reunification Efforts

Metro Schools provided updates on student transportation. By 2:15 p.m., buses began leaving the reunification site at 3754 Murfreesboro Pike. Students who drove to school would be taken back later to retrieve their vehicles.

Walkers were transported home if their parents couldn’t pick them up. Car riders reunited with their families at the designated site.

Former Student Not Surprised

Nadia Marquez, a former Antioch High student, said she was shocked when she first heard about the shooting but, upon reflection, found it unsurprising.

"There were always threats when I was in school," she said. "I just hoped everyone was okay."

While relieved that her boyfriend’s sister was safe, she acknowledged the emotional toll the event would take on students.

"Some kids are probably in shock or terrified. This kind of trauma stays with you," she said.

Lawmakers and Officials React

Various officials, including Tennessee’s federal representatives and Governor Bill Lee, expressed their condolences. However, Republican lawmakers did not propose any policy changes in response.

Senator Jeff Yarbro called for legislative action, emphasizing the need to create a safer environment for students.

"High school students should be able to eat lunch without fearing for their lives," he said.

Mayor O’Connell’s Statement

Nashville Mayor Freddie O’Connell described the shooting as a heartbreaking moment for the city.

"Our thoughts are with the families, students, and staff," he said. "We will support them however we can."

Community Mourns Another School Shooting

Voices for a Safer Tennessee, a nonprofit advocating against gun violence, expressed their sorrow over the incident.

"Schools should be safe spaces," the organization said in a statement. "We stand with the victims and their families."

Parents' Fear and Frustration

Many parents anxiously waited outside the reunification site. Chante Frye, whose ninth-grade daughter was inside during the shooting, described the experience as terrifying.

"It’s heartbreaking," she said. "And unfortunately, it’s not surprising given the rise in violence at schools."

Police Investigation Ongoing

Authorities continue to investigate the events leading up to the shooting, including whether warning signs were missed. Officers are urging anyone with relevant information to come forward.
