Reliance JioCoin beta testing begins to transform digital transactions in India


Introduction to JioCoin

Under the leadership of Mukesh Ambani, Jio Platforms has launched JioCoin, a blockchain-based reward token on the Polygon network. Integrated into JioSphere, the company’s web browser, JioCoin rewards users for online activities, sparking interest in its potential.

What is JioCoin?

JioCoin is a blockchain-powered token designed for Indian users. It can be earned by using Jio applications, with a focus on secure transactions and privacy protection. The token aims to enhance Jio’s ecosystem, spanning digital services, data storage, and e-commerce.

How Does JioCoin Work?


Users earn JioCoins by completing tasks within Jio applications. Similar to the Brave browser’s BAT system, users must be Indian residents aged 18 and above and join the BBRP program or its affiliates. Tokens are deposited into a Web3 wallet and can be redeemed for Jio services or exclusive offers.

Beta Testing and Launch

Currently in beta testing on the Jio Browser app, JioCoin’s full-scale launch has not been officially announced. The beta phase allows users to engage with the platform and provide feedback before the token's wider release.

Partnership with Polygon Labs

Reliance has collaborated with Polygon Labs to strengthen JioCoin’s blockchain framework. This partnership ensures the token’s scalability and operational efficiency, enhancing user experience.

Impact on India’s Crypto Market

JioCoin is expected to drive crypto adoption in India. By introducing blockchain technology and cryptocurrency to a larger audience, it opens up opportunities for other players to explore the Indian crypto ecosystem.
