 Maha Kumbh 2025: Basant Panchami on February 3; authorities gear up for flawless arrangements before the 'amrit snan'. 

The Maha Kumbh Mela 2025 in Prayagraj is set to witness the third and final Amrit Snan on Basant Panchami, falling on February 3. This sacred bath, considered highly auspicious, is expected to attract millions of devotees to the Triveni Sangam, where the holy rivers Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati converge. The event holds immense spiritual significance as it marks the last royal bath for the Naga Sadhus before the grand conclusion of the Kumbh Mela.

Following the tragic stampede on January 29 during Mauni Amavasya, which resulted in numerous casualties, authorities are leaving no stone unturned in ensuring a seamless and secure arrangement for the upcoming Amrit Snan. The Uttar Pradesh administration has intensified preparations, conducting high-level security reviews and crowd management assessments. Director General of Police Prashant Kumar and Chief Secretary Manoj Kumar Singh have led multiple inspections at the Kumbh site to evaluate the effectiveness of safety measures.

To prevent overcrowding and ensure the smooth movement of pilgrims, officials have implemented several strategic measures. All VIP movements have been suspended on February 3 to prioritize public safety and prevent unnecessary congestion. Additionally, vehicle entry restrictions will be reinstated on February 2 and 3, with traffic diversions in place to regulate the influx of devotees. Pilgrims are advised to follow designated routes and cooperate with law enforcement authorities.

Security has been significantly strengthened with the deployment of over 50,000 personnel, including local police, paramilitary forces, and disaster response teams. The entire Kumbh Mela area is under surveillance with more than 2,700 CCTV cameras monitoring crowd density and movement. Emergency response units have been stationed at key locations, and additional medical aid centers have been established to provide immediate assistance if required.

Officials have urged devotees to remain vigilant, follow safety advisories, and adhere to designated bathing zones to avoid overcrowding. With an estimated footfall of millions, authorities are committed to making this final Amrit Snan a spiritually fulfilling and incident-free experience for all participants. The Maha Kumbh 2025 continues to uphold its legacy as the world's largest religious gathering, uniting devotees in their pursuit of faith, devotion, and divine blessings.
