AK Mishra to meet ENPO today

Dimapur | January 14

AK Mishra Visits Nagaland for Frontier Nagaland Talks

Focus on Frontier Nagaland Territory (FNT)

AK Mishra, the Government of India’s representative for Indo-Naga talks, is in Nagaland to focus on the proposed Frontier Nagaland Territory (FNT) for the eastern region. His visit, starting January 14, is specifically for negotiations on this matter, led by the Eastern Nagaland People’s Organisation (ENPO).

Meeting with ENPO Delegation
Mishra arrived in Dimapur on January 14 and is scheduled to meet the ENPO delegation on January 15. The meeting will take place at the Nagaland Police Complex in Chümoukedima. Mishra is heading a three-member team from the Ministry of Home Affairs to engage with the ENPO regarding the FNT issue.

Courtesy Visit to NSCN (IM) Leader
Before the meeting, Mishra visited General Anthony Shimray, the army chief of the NSCN (IM), at his residence to offer condolences for the passing of Shimray’s wife. Kk

ENPO’s Statement on FNT
Ahead of the upcoming talks, the eight tribal bodies of the ENPO issued a statement. They emphasized that any solution to the Frontier Nagaland issue must include “Executive, Legislative, and Financial autonomy” for the region.

Request for Clarity from MHA
The ENPO’s Press and Media Cell urged the Ministry of Home Affairs to clarify its position on the FNT issue. The ENPO stressed that FNT was created by the Government of India, and its resolution should address the historical and political aspirations of the Eastern Nagas.

Call for Democratic Pursuit of FNT
The ENPO called on Eastern Nagas to pursue the FNT in a democratic manner and cautioned against the formation of the Eastern Naga Unemployed Youth Front.
