Bigger Screen and Enhanced Controllers

On Thursday, Nintendo unveiled its long-awaited Switch 2 hybrid console, which will be available later this year. The company promises a bigger screen and improved controllers, aiming to revitalize its hardware business.

The business showcased the Switch 2 in a video trailer. The clip starts with the current Switch model, before the two Joy-Con controllers detach and expand the tablet size they attach to. The controllers then appear to grow in size and transition to a matte black finish.

New Joy-Con Design and Magnetic Covers

The joysticks and controller covers come in light blue and red variations. The Joy-Con covers appear to attach magnetically when detached from the main Switch 2 tablet. Users can also prop up the console on its stand or dock it to play on a home TV.

Improved Kickstand and Similar Design to Leaks

One notable enhancement is the improved U-shaped kickstand, which allows the console to be placed further back, almost flat on a surface. The overall design of the Switch 2 closely resembles previous mock-ups leaked online ahead of its official announcement.

Backward Compatibility and Upcoming Event

Nintendo confirmed that the Switch 2 is backward compatible, meaning users can play existing Switch games on the new model. However, a specific release date has not yet been announced. More details will be revealed during the next Nintendo Direct online event on April 2.

Conclusion

The Switch 2 is shaping up to be a significant upgrade with its bigger screen, enhanced controllers, and improved kickstand. With backward compatibility and an upcoming Nintendo Direct event, fans eagerly await more details. Nintendo hopes that this new release will reignite interest in its hardware market and counter its recent financial decline.
