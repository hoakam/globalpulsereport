Gang leader shot dead in court by attacker posing as a lawyer.

 A notorious gang leader was fatally shot inside a Sri Lankan courthouse by a gunman disguised as a lawyer, according to police. The attacker used a revolver that had been smuggled into the building inside a hollowed-out book by a female accomplice, who remains on the run.

The victim, identified as Sanjeewa Kumara Samararathne—also known as Ganemulle Sanjeewa—was in court for legal proceedings when the shooting occurred. Authorities say he was under investigation for multiple murder cases and had been in custody since his arrest in September 2023.

The dramatic attack took place in Colombo, the nation’s capital, and is part of an ongoing cycle of gang-related killings that officials have been struggling to curb. Despite pledges from the government to crack down on organized crime, such violent incidents continue to persist.

Police officers had escorted Samararathne to court with a security detail of about a dozen personnel. After being shot, he was rushed to a hospital but was pronounced dead upon arrival. The shooter initially managed to escape but was later apprehended. Authorities say he used multiple aliases, leading to some discrepancies in identification. 

The female suspect, identified as 25-year-old Pinpura Dewage Ishara Sewwandi, is still at large. Police have announced a reward for any information that could lead to her capture. Additionally, a police officer and a van driver suspected of assisting the perpetrators have been taken into custody.

The incident has sparked serious discussions in parliament regarding gang-related violence, with lawmakers raising concerns about security measures in courts. An opposition MP described the issue as a "major security threat."

Health and Mass Media Minister Nalinda Jayatissa, who previously vowed to tackle organized crime, reaffirmed on Wednesday that the government is taking these threats seriously. In response to the courthouse shooting, new security protocols are being introduced, including the deployment of armed guards during high-risk court appearances. Typically, armed personnel are not permitted inside courtrooms, Justice Minister Harshana Nanayakkara stated on Thursday.

So far in 2025, at least nine people have lost their lives due to gang-related shootings, according to police data cited by AFP. Authorities continue to tighten security as they seek to prevent further violence.
