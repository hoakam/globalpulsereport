Maha Kumbh Live:Basant Panchami 'Amrit Snan' Takes Place Amid Tightened Security

Mahakumbh Live: Devotees were greeted with a shower of flower petals as they performed their sacred bathing rituals.

The Maha Kumbh Mela is witnessing another sacred 'Amrit Snan' on Monday, coinciding with the festival of Basant Panchami. This marks the first major holy bathing event following the tragic stampede that occurred on the last significant bathing day, which resulted in the deaths of at least 30 people. To prevent any untoward incidents, authorities have intensified security measures and established a structured bathing sequence for various spiritual sects at the Sangam Nose—the site of last week’s fatal crowd surge.

Key Highlights of the Amrit Snan

The event marks the third 'Amrit Snan' of the ongoing Kumbh Mela. Before sunrise, numerous saints and seers from different ‘akharas’ participated in the sacred ritual by immersing themselves in the holy waters. By 4 AM, an estimated 16.58 lakh devotees had already taken the dip, and the Uttar Pradesh government anticipates that the total number of pilgrims bathing on Monday could reach 5 crore.

  • Significance of Basant Panchami: This Hindu festival, observed on the first day of spring, falls on the fifth day of the Magha month. It also marks the beginning of Holi preparations and is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Saraswati.

  • Stampede Tragedy at the Previous Amrit Snan: The last major bathing event, held on Mauni Amavasya, witnessed a tragic stampede that claimed 30 lives and left 60 others injured. In response to this unfortunate incident, Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath has been personally overseeing the preparations since Saturday.

  • Enhanced Security Measures: The state government has significantly strengthened security arrangements, deployed additional medical teams, and introduced improved crowd management strategies to prevent a recurrence of such incidents.

The Maha Kumbh Mela, which takes place once every 12 years, commenced on January 13, 2025, and will conclude on February 26. The remaining significant bathing dates include February 12 (Maghi Purnima) and February 26 (Maha Shivaratri).
