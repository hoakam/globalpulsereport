The Hornbill Festival is a vibrant cultural festival held annually in Nagaland, a northeastern state of India. Named after the hornbill, a revered bird in Naga folklore, the festival is a showcase of the rich cultural heritage, traditions, and customs of the various tribes of Nagaland.

Key Features of the Hornbill Festival:

  1. Dates: It is celebrated every year from December 1 to December 10 to coincide with Nagaland's Statehood Day on December 1.

  2. Location: The main venue is the Naga Heritage Village, located at Kisama, about 12 km from Kohima, the capital of Nagaland.

  3. Purpose:

    • To promote and preserve the culture of the Naga tribes.
    • To foster unity among the tribes of Nagaland.
    • To attract tourists and showcase the state's traditions globally.

  4. Activities:

    • Traditional Dances and Music: Performances by tribal groups in their traditional attire.
    • Ethnic Cuisine: A variety of Naga and tribal delicacies.
    • Handicrafts and Art: Exhibition and sale of traditional crafts, art, and jewelry.
    • Sports and Competitions: Traditional games like wrestling, archery, and more.
    • Rock Festival: A popular rock music event attracting bands from across the country.
    • Cultural Programs: Storytelling, fashion shows, and cultural seminars.
    • Night Bazaar: A vibrant marketplace with food, crafts, and souvenirs.

  5. Tourism and Cultural Exchange:

    • The festival draws tourists from India and abroad.
    • It serves as a platform for cultural exchange and understanding among communities.

The Hornbill Festival is often called the "Festival of Festivals" due to its comprehensive celebration of Naga life and traditions, making it one of the most colorful and significant cultural festivals in India.


The Hornbill Festival in Nagaland has become a significant attraction for both domestic and international visitors, with attendance numbers increasing annually.

Recent Attendance Statistics:

  • 2024: The festival recorded over 1.73 lakh (173,000) attendees, including 2,375 international visitors from countries such as the UK, Wales, Peru, the USA, and Japan.

  • 2023: Approximately 1.54 lakh (154,000) visitors attended, with 2,108 being foreigners.

  • 2022: The festival attracted around 1.40 lakh (140,000) attendees, including 1,026 foreign visitors.

Notable Visitors:

The festival has been graced by various dignitaries and international guests.

  • 2024: The event was inaugurated by Nagaland Governor La Ganesan and Chief Minister Neiphiu Rio, with representatives from partner countries such as the UK, Wales, Peru, the USA, and Japan in attendance.

  • 2023: The festival saw participation from international visitors, including those from the UK, Wales, Peru, the USA, and Japan.

The increasing number of visitors each year underscores the festival's growing popularity and its significance as a cultural showcase on both national and international platforms.


The Hornbill Festival in Nagaland is a vibrant celebration of the state's rich tribal culture, traditions, and heritage. The festival features a wide array of activities that showcase the diversity and unity of the Naga tribes. Here's an overview of what typically happens during the festival:

Daily Activities and Highlights: 



1. Cultural Performances:
  • Traditional dances and folk music by Naga tribes.
  • Performances in colorful traditional attire that reflect tribal myths, folklore, and customs.

2. Art and Craft Exhibitions:

  • Display and sale of handmade crafts, jewelry, wood carvings, and textiles.
  • Demonstrations of traditional weaving and pottery techniques.

3. Ethnic Cuisine:

  • Stalls offering authentic Naga food like smoked pork, bamboo shoot dishes, and fermented delicacies.
  • Local drinks like Zutho (rice beer) and freshly brewed tea.

4. Sports and Games:

  • Traditional Naga games such as:
    • Naga wrestling (a popular sport among the tribes).
    • Archery competitions.
  • Indigenous skills contests like fire-making and war cry competitions.

5. Music and Entertainment:

  • A Rock Music Festival, one of Asia's biggest rock music events.
  • Live concerts featuring both local and international artists.

6. Night Bazaar:

  • A lively marketplace with food, crafts, and souvenirs.
  • Open-air entertainment and community interaction.

7. Workshops and Seminars:

  • Discussions on Naga history, culture, and environmental preservation.
  • Art and storytelling workshops for children and adults.

Special Events and Themes: 

1. Unity Dance and Parade:

  • A spectacular opening ceremony with a parade of all participating tribes.
  • Unity dances symbolize the cultural harmony of Nagaland.

2. Hornbill International Film Festival:

  • Screening of films and documentaries focused on tribal life, folklore, and the environment.

3. Adventure Activities:

  • Trekking, biking, and other adventure sports in the scenic Naga hills.
  • Traditional hunting demonstrations in controlled settings.

4. Fashion Shows:

  • Showcasing traditional and contemporary Naga fashion.
  • Blending indigenous designs with modern trends.

5. Indigenous Games Competitions:

  • Contests like traditional log-drumming, chili-eating, and war cry demonstrations.

Purpose of the Festival:

  • Celebrate and preserve Naga heritage.
  • Promote tourism and cultural exchange.
  • Offer a platform for tribal artisans, performers, and entrepreneurs.

The Hornbill Festival is a grand confluence of tradition and modernity, attracting visitors from across the world to experience the vibrant culture of Nagaland.
