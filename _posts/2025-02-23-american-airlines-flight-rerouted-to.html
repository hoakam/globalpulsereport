American Airlines flight rerouted to Rome following a bomb threat.


An American Airlines flight traveling from New York to New Delhi was unexpectedly diverted to Rome on Sunday due to a reported bomb threat, according to sources familiar with the matter.

American Airlines confirmed in a statement that Flight AA292, which departed from John F. Kennedy International Airport with a scheduled destination of Indira Gandhi International Airport, was redirected to Leonardo da Vinci Rome Fiumicino Airport following a potential security concern. After a thorough assessment, authorities determined that the threat was not credible. As part of standard security procedures at Indira Gandhi International Airport, a precautionary inspection was required before the aircraft could continue its journey.

Reports suggest the bomb threat was communicated via email. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) stated that the flight crew swiftly reported the security concern, prompting the necessary response measures.

"Ensuring the safety and security of our passengers and crew is our top priority, and we regret any inconvenience this may have caused," American Airlines said in its statement.

Around 2:00 p.m. local time, the flight made a formal request for diversion to Rome, as confirmed by Roberto Rao, a spokesperson for Leonardo da Vinci International Airport.

“We promptly approved the request and arranged for a secure landing,” Rao told NBC News. “Although we were not given specific details about the security threat, it was considered serious enough to necessitate a diversion, but not an immediate emergency, as the alert was received while the aircraft was over the Caspian Sea, approximately three hours from Rome.”

As the aircraft entered Italian airspace, it was escorted by two Italian Air Force fighter jets before safely landing at Leonardo da Vinci International Airport at approximately 5:30 p.m. local time, according to the FAA.

Upon arrival, law enforcement and security teams carried out a comprehensive inspection of the plane. Following clearance from authorities, the flight was scheduled to resume its journey to New Delhi the following day, allowing the crew to rest before continuing operations.
