 CM Rio reviews progress at Konyak heritage village in Mon.

Chief Minister Neiphiu Rio recently visited the Konyak heritage village in Mon district

Nagaland Chief Minister Neiphiu Rio recently visited the Konyak heritage village in Mon district to inspect the ongoing development work aimed at preserving and showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Konyak community. Expressing optimism about the project, Rio shared on X that he looked forward to the site becoming a center for community unity and cultural pride, where people could strengthen their bonds.

As per a report from the Department of Information and Public Relations (DIPR), the chief minister was accompanied by the Minister of Forests, Environment & Climate Change and Village Guard, CL John, along with Nagaland Democratic Progressive Party (NDPP) president Chingwang Konyak. Addressing those present at the heritage site, Rio extended his gratitude to the administration, civil society organizations, landowners, and workers for their contributions to the project so far.

Emphasizing that unity and cooperation are crucial for societal development, Rio urged the local population to continue their support to ensure the successful completion of the heritage village. He also stressed the importance of quality control in the construction process and reminded relevant departments to ensure proper electricity and water supply connectivity to the site. According to him, all construction work is expected to be completed within the next two months, with the inauguration scheduled for the first week of April 2025.

During the visit, Konyak Union (KU) president Tingtok Konyak welcomed the gathering, while Mon Deputy Commissioner Ajit Kumar Verma extended his gratitude to the chief minister and his team for their visit. A brief speech was also delivered by representatives of the Konyak Heritage Management Community, highlighting the cultural and historical significance of the project.
