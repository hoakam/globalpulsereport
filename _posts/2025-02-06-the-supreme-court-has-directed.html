The Supreme Court has directed protection for Discovery staff following threats related to the documentary Cult of Fear: Asaram Bapu.

 On February 6, the Supreme Court of India granted interim police protection to the officials and property of Discovery Communications India due to threats arising from the broadcaster’s documentary about self-styled godman Asaram Bapu.

The bench, consisting of Chief Justice of India Sanjiv Khanna and Justice Sanjay Kumar, was hearing a plea filed by Discovery India's top officials. The plea highlighted that following the release of the documentary Cult of Fear: Asaram Bapu, the channel and its associates faced several hate comments and threats on social media.

Senior Advocate Abhinav Mukherji, representing the petitioners, argued that the threats had made it increasingly difficult for the Discovery officials to travel freely across the country. He stated, "The police have done nothing; we went home and asked employees not to come to work. We’ve now received a letter threatening us with a mass agitation. There’s a 2018 guideline that Lordships have laid down," referring to an existing safety protocol.

The Supreme Court bench issued an order directing the authorities to provide necessary protection, stating, "Issue notice returnable in the week commencing March 3, 2025. In the meantime, we request the police to ensure the petitioners can use their office without fear of physical harm."

The petition, filed under Article 32 of the Constitution, named the Union Government (through the Ministry of Home Affairs) as well as the police heads of various states, including Karnataka, Maharashtra, West Bengal, Delhi, Haryana, and Telangana. The petitioners argued that the documentary, which was based on public records, court rulings, and witness testimonies, was made with journalistic integrity.

The petitioners also recounted an incident on January 30, when a mob gathered outside their Mumbai office, causing a disturbance. Although the police dispersed the crowd, no action was taken against the individuals responsible.
