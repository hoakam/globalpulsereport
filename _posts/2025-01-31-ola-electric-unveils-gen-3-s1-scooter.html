Ola Electric Unveils Gen 3 S1 Scooter Lineup, Prices Begin at ₹79,999


Ola Electric has taken a major step in strengthening its presence in India’s electric vehicle market by launching its third-generation S1 series. The company introduced eight new electric scooters, spanning various price categories, with prices starting at ₹79,999 and going up to ₹1,69,999.

Enhanced Performance and Advanced Technology

The highlight of the new lineup is the flagship S1 Pro+, featuring a cutting-edge 5.3kWh battery utilizing Ola’s proprietary 4680 Bharat Cell technology. This premium model delivers an impressive range of up to 320 km and a top speed of 141 kmph. For those looking for a more budget-friendly premium experience, a 4kWh variant is also available.

Bhavish Aggarwal, Ola Electric’s Chairman and Managing Director, described the new generation of scooters as a “significant leap forward” in electric mobility. "Our focus goes beyond just improving efficiency and performance—we aim to redefine what’s possible in the electric two-wheeler industry," he stated.

The Gen 3 platform brings a host of engineering enhancements, including a mid-drive motor, a chain drive system, and an integrated Motor Control Unit. These innovations contribute to a 20% boost in peak power and range while reducing costs by 11% compared to previous models.

Affordable Options for Mass-Market Buyers

For budget-conscious consumers, Ola has introduced the S1 X series, with the entry-level S1 X (2kWh) priced at ₹79,999. The range extends to the S1 X+, which costs ₹1,07,999 and offers more advanced features and better performance.

Industry-First Safety and Software Innovations

Ola has set new benchmarks in safety by incorporating the first-ever dual ABS system in electric scooters. Additionally, the introduction of a patented brake-by-wire system ensures a more balanced approach to regenerative and mechanical braking, increasing energy recovery by 15% while enhancing rider safety.

On the software front, Ola Electric plans to release the MoveOS 5 beta in mid-February. This update will bring features like a Smartwatch App, Smart Park, and an upgraded Ola Maps navigation system.

Future Expansion Plans and Manufacturing Strength

The company is also gearing up for future product launches, including the Gig and S1 Z series, which will cater to both personal and commercial users, with prices starting from ₹39,999. Additionally, Ola Electric plans to enter the motorcycle market with its Roadster series, featuring models ranging from ₹74,999 to ₹1,99,999.

To support its growing lineup, Ola Electric operates India’s largest company-owned EV retail network, with over 800 experience centers nationwide. Its manufacturing hub, the Ola Futurefactory in Tamil Nadu, is complemented by its Battery Innovation Centre in Bengaluru and R&D facilities in India, the UK, and the US.

Deliveries and Warranty Details

The newly launched Gen 3 scooters will begin deliveries in mid-February. Every model comes with a standard 3-year/40,000 km warranty covering both the scooter and battery. Customers can also opt for an extended battery warranty of up to 8 years or 1,25,000 km for an additional ₹14,999.
