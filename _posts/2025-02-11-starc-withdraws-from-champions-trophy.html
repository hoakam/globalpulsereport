Starc withdraws from Champions Trophy due to personal reasons; Smith appointed as Australia's captain.

              Starc has opted out of the tournament for private reasons, with Cricket Australia showing their support for the left-arm seame


Australia's preparations for the upcoming ICC Champions Trophy have encountered significant challenges, with key players withdrawing due to injuries and personal reasons. The latest setback is the withdrawal of fast bowler Mitchell Starc, who has cited personal reasons for his decision. This development leaves the Australian squad without its three primary pace bowlers, as Pat Cummins and Josh Hazlewood have already been sidelined due to injuries.

In response to these absences, the selection committee has appointed Steve Smith as the captain for the tournament. Smith recently led Australia to a 2-0 Test series victory against Sri Lanka, showcasing his leadership capabilities. The squad will now rely on a group of less experienced fast bowlers, including Sean Abbott, Spencer Johnson, Ben Dwarshuis, and Nathan Ellis, to spearhead the bowling attack.

The batting lineup has also seen adjustments due to the unavailability of all-rounders Mitchell Marsh, who is recovering from a back injury, and Marcus Stoinis, who recently announced his retirement from One Day Internationals. To bolster the batting order, selectors have included Jake Fraser-McGurk, who may occupy the number three position, with Matt Short and Travis Head likely to open the innings. Leg-spinner Tanveer Sangha has also been added to the 15-man squad, bringing additional variety to the bowling department.

Australia's campaign in the Champions Trophy is set to commence against England in Lahore on February 22. The team will subsequently face South Africa and Afghanistan in the group stages. Despite the recent setbacks, the squad remains a blend of seasoned players and newcomers eager to make their mark on the international stage.

The finalized Australian squad for the Champions Trophy is as follows: Steve Smith (captain), Sean Abbott, Alex Carey, Ben Dwarshuis, Nathan Ellis, Jake Fraser-McGurk, Aaron Hardie, Travis Head, Josh Inglis, Spencer Johnson, Marnus Labuschagne, Glenn Maxwell, Tanveer Sangha, Matthew Short, and Adam Zampa. Cooper Connolly has been named as the traveling reserve.

As the tournament approaches, the Australian team will be keen to overcome these hurdles and perform strongly, aiming to add another ICC trophy to their illustrious cricketing history.
