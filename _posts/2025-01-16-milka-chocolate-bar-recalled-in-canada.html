Milka Chocolate Bar Pulled from Canadian Market Due to Undeclared Hazelnuts


CFIA Issues Nationwide Recall

A chocolate bar sold by the Swiss company Milka has been recalled across Canada due to undeclared hazelnuts, according to the Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA).

Product Details and Recall Reason

The affected product, known as Milka Bubbly Alpine Milk, is being removed from the marketplace because hazelnut is not declared on its label. The CFIA issued a recall notice on Tuesday, warning consumers about the potential health risks for those with nut allergies.

Distribution Across Canada

The recalled Milka chocolate bars were distributed in multiple provinces, including:

  • Alberta

  • British Columbia

  • New Brunswick

  • Newfoundland and Labrador

  • Nova Scotia

  • Ontario

  • Quebec

Additionally, the product is available for purchase on Amazon.

Consumer Advisory


The CFIA has urged consumers to either dispose of the recalled product or return it to the place of purchase. Individuals with hazelnut allergies risk serious allergic reactions if they consume the chocolate.

Mondelez Internationalâ€™s Response

A spokesperson for Mondelez International Inc., which owns the Milka brand, clarified that the product was not imported or sold by Mondelez Canada.

The recall notice states that the chocolate bars were distributed by Bensus Imports, an unauthorized distributor. The incorrect labeling, which led to the undeclared allergen, was applied by the distributor.

CFIA Recall Classification

The CFIA classified this as a Class II recall, indicating a moderate health risk. Consumers should check for the product name and distributor details before consuming Milka chocolates in Canada.

For more information, visit the CFIA website.
