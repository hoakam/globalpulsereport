 Best Tourist Places To Visit In Indian States:-



India is a vast and diverse country with unique attractions in every state. Here’s a curated list of the best tourist destinations in each Indian state:

1. Andhra Pradesh



  • Tirupati: Home to the famous Sri Venkateswara Temple.
  • Araku Valley: Scenic hill station with coffee plantations and waterfalls.
  • Visakhapatnam (Vizag): Beaches like RK Beach, Kailasagiri Hill, and the Submarine Museum.

2. Arunachal Pradesh



  • Tawang: Known for Tawang Monastery, the largest in India.
  • Ziro Valley: Famous for the Ziro Music Festival and lush paddy fields.
  • Namdapha National Park: Rich in biodiversity.

3. Assam



  • Kaziranga National Park: Home to the one-horned rhinoceros.
  • Majuli: World’s largest river island with unique culture and satras (monasteries).
  • Guwahati: Kamakhya Temple and Brahmaputra River cruises.

4. Bihar



  • Bodh Gaya: Sacred Buddhist site with the Mahabodhi Temple.
  • Nalanda: Ancient university ruins and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Rajgir: Hot springs and spiritual significance.

5. Chhattisgarh



  • Chitrakote Falls: Known as the "Niagara of India."
  • Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary: Rich in flora and fauna.
  • Bastar: Known for tribal culture and Danteshwari Temple.

6. Goa



  • Beaches: Baga, Calangute, and Palolem are popular.
  • Old Goa: Churches like Basilica of Bom Jesus (UNESCO site).
  • Dudhsagar Falls: Stunning waterfalls in the Western Ghats.

7. Gujarat



  • Gir National Park: Home to the Asiatic lions.
  • Rann of Kutch: Famous for the white salt desert and Rann Utsav.
  • Somnath Temple: One of the 12 Jyotirlingas.

8. Haryana



  • Kurukshetra: Historical significance related to the Mahabharata.
  • Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary: A paradise for bird watchers.
  • Pinjore Gardens: Beautiful Mughal-style gardens.

9. Himachal Pradesh



  • Shimla: Colonial charm and scenic beauty.
  • Manali: Adventure sports and Solang Valley.
  • Spiti Valley: Stunning landscapes and monasteries.

10. Jammu & Kashmir




  • Srinagar: Dal Lake, Mughal Gardens, and houseboats.
  • Leh-Ladakh: Breathtaking landscapes and monasteries.
  • Gulmarg: Known for skiing and cable car rides.

11. Jharkhand



  • Ranchi: Waterfalls like Dassam and Hundru Falls.
  • Netarhat: Known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur."
  • Betla National Park: Rich wildlife and scenic landscapes.

12. Karnataka



  • Mysore: Mysore Palace and Brindavan Gardens.
  • Coorg: Coffe
  • e plantations and Abbey Falls.
  • Hampi: UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient ruins.

13. Kerala



  • Munnar: Tea plantations and Eravikulam National Park.
  • Alleppey: Backwaters and houseboats.
  • Thekkady: Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary.

14. Madhya Pradesh



  • Khajuraho: Famous for erotic sculptures and temples (UNESCO site).
  • Bandhavgarh National Park: Known for tigers.
  • Ujjain: Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga and Kumbh Mela site.

15. Maharashtra



  • Mumbai: Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and Bollywood tours.
  • Ajanta & Ellora Caves: UNESCO World Heritage Sites with ancient art.
  • Mahabaleshwar: Hill station and strawberry farms.

16. Manipur



  • Loktak Lake: The largest freshwater lake in northeast India.
  • Imphal: Kangla Fort and Ima Keithel market.
  • Ukhrul: Famous for the Shirui Lily.

17. Meghalaya



  • Shillong: Scotland of the East with waterfalls and lakes.
  • Cherrapunj
  • i: Living root bridges and Nohkalikai Falls.
  • Mawsynram: The wettest place on Earth.

18. Mizoram



  • Aizawl: Scenic city with cultural attractions.
  • Reiek: Ideal for trekking and stunning views.
  • Phawngpui: Known as the "Blue Mountain."

19.  Nagaland



  • Kohima: War Cemetery and Hornbill Festival.
  • Dimapur: Ruins of ancient Kachari Kingdom.
  • Dzükou Valley: Famous for trekking and natural beauty.

20. Odisha



  • Puri: Jagannath Temple and Puri Beach.
  • Konark: Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Chilika Lake: Asia's largest brackish water lagoon.

21. Punjab



  • Amrits
  • ar: Golden Temple and Wagah Border.
  • Anandpur Sahib: A holy Sikh pilgrimage site.
  • Patiala: Known for its palaces and gardens.

22. Rajasthan



  • Jaipur: Amer Fort, Hawa Mahal, and Pink City charm.
  • Udaipur: City of Lakes and palaces.
  • Jaisalmer: Thar Desert and Golden Fort.

23. Sikkim



  • Gangtok: Nathu La Pass and Tsomgo Lake.
  • Pelling: Views of Mount Kanchenjunga.
  • Lachen and Lachung: Scenic beauty and snow-capped peaks.

24. Tamil Nadu



  • Chennai: Marina Beach and Kapaleeshwarar Temple.
  • Ooty: Hill station with tea gardens.
  • Mahabalipuram: Rock-cut temples and beaches.

25. Telangana



  • Hyderabad: Charminar, Golconda Fort, and Ramoji Film City.
  • Warangal: Thousand Pillar Temple and Warangal Fort.
  • Nagarjuna Sagar: A massive dam and scenic beauty.

26. Tripura



  • Agartala: Ujjayanta Palace and Neermahal.
  • Unakoti: Ancient rock carvings.
  • Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary: Rich biodiversity.

27. Uttar Pradesh



  • Agra: Taj Mahal and Agra Fort.
  • Varanasi: Spiritual city on the Ganges.
  • Lucknow: Bara Imambara and Rumi Darwaza.

28. Uttarakhand



  • Rishikesh: Yoga capital and adventure activities.
  • Nainital: Scenic lake and hill views.
  • Jim Corbett National Park: Oldest national park in India.

29. West Bengal



  • Darjeeling: Tea plantations and the Toy Train.
  • Kolkata: Victoria Memorial and Howrah Bridge.
  • Sundarbans: Mangrove forests and Royal Bengal Tigers.

This list showcases the best each state offers, making India an incredible destination for travelers!
