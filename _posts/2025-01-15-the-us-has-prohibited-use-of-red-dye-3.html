The US has prohibited the use of Red Dye 3 in certain candies, frosting, and medicines.


Overview of the Ban

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has banned the use of Red No 3, a synthetic dye used to give foods and beverages a bright cherry-red hue. The decision follows studies linking the dye to cancer in male laboratory rats.

Although human exposure levels are lower, U.S. law mandates a ban if any carcinogenic link is discovered.

Affected Products

Red Dye 3 is widely used in:

  • Candy, cakes, and cookies
  • Frozen desserts and frosting
  • Maraschino cherries and fruit-flavored drinks
  • Cough syrups and gummy vitamins

Advocacy and Legal History

The ban stems from a 2022 petition by advocacy groups, including the Center for Science in the Public Interest (CSPI), citing health risks and its use in children’s foods.

  • Banned in cosmetics by the FDA 35 years ago.
  • California outlawed it in food products in 2023.
  • Restricted in regions like the EU, Australia, and New Zealand.

Timeline for Compliance

  • Food Manufacturers: Reformulate products by January 2027.
  • Drug Manufacturers: Deadline extended to January 2028.
  • Imports: Must comply with the ban.

Which Foods Contain Red Dye 3? 

Commonly found in:

  • Colored candies like Pez
  • Toaster pastries and sodas
  • Gummy vitamins and syrups

Alternatives and Concerns

Some companies have shifted to Red Dye 40, seen as safer but linked to bowel disorders in mice and hyperactivity in children. California schools banned it in 2023.

What’s Next? 

The FDA will monitor compliance as manufacturers reformulate products, ensuring consumer safety.
