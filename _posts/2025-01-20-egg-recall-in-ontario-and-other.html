Egg Recall in Ontario and Other Provinces Due to Salmonella

Burnbrae Farms Issues Nationwide Recall

Eggs sold in Ontario, British Columbia, Manitoba, and possibly other provinces have been recalled due to potential salmonella contamination. The recall, issued by Burnbrae Farms, affects multiple brands, including Compliments, Foremost, Golden Valley Eggs, IGA, No Name, and Western Family.

 

Retailers and Affected Products

The recalled eggs were sold at major grocery chains such as Walmart, Loblaw, Sobeys, and No Frills. The affected products have best-before dates of February 16, February 22, and March 1.

CFIA Warning and Safety Measures

The Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) issued a Class 2 recall after testing identified the presence of salmonella. Although no reported illnesses have been linked to the recall, the CFIA is investigating the issue and ensuring that the recalled products are removed from store shelves.

Health officials warn that contaminated eggs may not look or smell spoiled but can cause serious illness, particularly in young children, pregnant women, seniors, and those with weakened immune systems. Symptoms of salmonella infection include:

 


  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Vomiting
  • Nausea
  • Stomach cramps
  • Diarrhea
  • In severe cases, long-term complications such as arthritis

Consumer Advisory

The CFIA advises consumers not to eat, serve, or sell the recalled eggs. Those who have purchased affected batches should dispose of them immediately or return them to the store for a refund. Anyone who experiences symptoms after consuming the recalled eggs should seek medical attention.

Next Steps

Consumers are urged to check their egg cartons for the lot numbers and UPCs listed above. If you have any of the recalled eggs in your home, discard them or return them to the place of purchase.

For more information, visit the CFIA website or contact your local health authorities.
