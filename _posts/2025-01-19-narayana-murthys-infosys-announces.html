Narayana Murthy’s Infosys Announces Salary Hike for Employees

Employees in India to Receive 6-8% Pay Increase

Infosys, co-founded by Narayana Murthy, has announced a salary hike of 6-8% for employees in India as part of its phased salary revision plan. The increments will take effect in January 2025, with a second phase scheduled for April 2025.

Lower Salary Hikes for Overseas Employees

While employees in India will benefit from a higher percentage hike, those based outside the country will receive lower, single-digit increments. This follows the company's cautious approach to compensation adjustments amid global economic uncertainties.

CFO Jayesh Sanghrajka’s Statement

During the company’s Q3FY25 results briefing, Infosys' Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Jayesh Sanghrajka, confirmed the salary hike, stating:

“Broadly, we expect 6-8% increments in India, and overseas adjustments will follow earlier patterns.”

Infosys, which employs over 3.23 lakh professionals, had delayed salary hikes to the fourth quarter of FY25 due to slower IT spending and global challenges. The last salary revision was implemented in November 2023. 

Impact on Profitability and Future Outlook

Infosys has not disclosed the exact impact of these wage hikes on its profit margins. However, Sanghrajka warned of possible headwinds in Q4FY25 and Q1FY26 due to increased employee costs.

CEO Salil Parekh assured that high-performing employees would receive higher salary increments, reinforcing the company’s commitment to retaining top talent.

Despite economic uncertainties, Infosys delivered strong financial results in Q3FY25:

  • Net Profit: Rs 6,806 crore, reflecting an 11.4% year-on-year increase
  • Revenue from Operations: Rs 41,764 crore, up 7.5% from Rs 38,821 crore in Q3FY24

With this salary revision, Infosys aims to boost employee morale and retain top talent while navigating the challenges of the global IT industry.
